BattlePicks DFS Contest Rules

Effective Date: [DATE]

These DFS Contest Rules ("Rules") govern all Daily Fantasy Sports contests ("Contests") offered on the BattlePicks platform. These Rules are part of and subject to the BattlePicks Terms of Service. By entering a Contest, you agree to these Rules.

1. Contest Format: Props Battle

BattlePicks offers peer-to-peer DFS pick'em Contests called "Props Battle." In a Props Battle, two Users compete head-to-head by selecting predictions on individual player statistical performances.

2. Entry Requirements

2.1 Pick Structure

Each Contest entry requires the following:

  • A minimum of three (3) Picks on individual player statistical performances (e.g., points scored, rebounds, assists)
  • Each Pick must be on a different player (one Pick per player maximum)
  • Picks must include players from at least two (2) different teams
  • Each Pick consists of selecting "Over" or "Under" on a player's projected statistical line for a specific game

2.2 Entry Fees

Contest entry fees are denominated in USD. Available entry amounts are displayed in the App and may include $5, $25, and $50 tiers. BattlePicks reserves the right to modify available entry amounts at any time.

2.3 Supported Sports and Markets

BattlePicks currently supports player prop markets for the following sports and statistical categories:

NBA: - Player Points (Over/Under) - Player Rebounds (Over/Under) - Player Assists (Over/Under) - Alternate lines for Points, Rebounds, and Assists (where available)

Additional sports and statistical categories may be added in the future. Available markets depend on third-party data availability and may vary by game and day.

2.4 Odds and Lines

All statistical lines and associated odds displayed on the platform are sourced from licensed third-party sportsbooks via The Odds API. BattlePicks does not create, set, or modify any odds or lines.

Lines and odds are locked at the time of Contest entry submission and do not change after submission, regardless of subsequent line movement.

3. Matching

3.1 Automatic Matching

Contest entries are matched automatically by the platform. Users cannot select, challenge, or request a specific opponent. Entries are matched based on contest format, currency, and entry amount.

3.2 Overlap Rules

When two Users are matched, no more than one (1) player may overlap between their entries. This means at least two of your three Picks must be on players that your opponent did not select.

3.3 Entry Expiration

If no opponent is found before the earliest game in your entry is scheduled to begin, your entry will be automatically canceled and your entry fee refunded in full. Refunded amounts are subject to the playthrough requirement as described in the Terms of Service.

4. Scoring

4.1 Points System

Each Pick is scored using a points system based on the decimal odds (payout multiplier) of the selected line at the time the entry was locked.

  • If a Pick is correct (the player's actual stat meets the Over/Under prediction), the Pick earns points equal to its decimal odds value. For example, a Pick at -110 American odds equals 1.91 decimal odds and earns 1.91 points if correct.
  • If a Pick is incorrect, the Pick earns 0 points.

4.2 Total Score

A User's total score is the sum of points earned across all Picks in the Contest. The User with the higher total score wins the Contest.

4.3 Examples

Player A Over 25.5 PTS -110 1.91 Correct (scored 28) 1.91

5. Settlement

5.1 Automatic Settlement

Contests are settled automatically after all games included in the Contest have been completed and final official player statistics are available from our data providers.

5.2 Prize Distribution

The winner of a Contest receives the combined entry fees of both Users, minus a 5% platform fee ("Rake").

Example: Two Users each enter $10. Total pool = $20. Rake (5%) = $1.00. Winner receives $19.00.

5.3 Ties

If both Users score the same total points, the Contest is declared a tie. Both Users receive a full refund of their entry fee. No Rake is charged on tied Contests. Refunded amounts are subject to the playthrough requirement.

6. Edge Cases and Special Situations

6.1 Did Not Play (DNP)

If a player included in a Pick does not participate in the game for any reason (injury scratch, coach's decision, rest day, or any other reason), that Pick is scored as a Push and earns 1.0 points for both Users. The remainder of the Contest is scored and settled normally.

6.2 Player Injury During Game

If a player is injured during the course of a game and leaves before it ends, the Pick is graded based on the player's final official statistical line for that game. No special adjustment is made for partial games.

6.3 Postponed or Canceled Games

If a game is postponed or canceled and no official final statistics are available within 24 hours of the originally scheduled start time, the affected Pick is voided and scored as a Push (1.0 points). The remainder of the Contest is scored and settled normally.

If the postponed game is played within the 24-hour window and official statistics become available, the Pick will be graded based on those statistics.

6.4 Missing Statistical Data

If official statistical data for a completed game is not available from our data providers within 24 hours after game completion, the affected Pick may be placed under manual review by BattlePicks. We will make reasonable efforts to obtain accurate statistics and grade the Pick. Users will be notified if their Contest is under review.

6.5 All Picks Voided

If all Picks in a Contest are voided (e.g., due to multiple game cancellations), both Users receive a full refund of their entry fee. No Rake is charged. Refunded amounts are subject to the playthrough requirement.

6.6 Push on Exact Line

If a player's final statistic exactly equals the line (e.g., the line is Over 25.0 and the player scores exactly 25), the Pick is scored as a Push and earns 1.0 points. Note: most lines include a half-point (e.g., 25.5) which eliminates the possibility of a push on that Pick.

7. Data Sources

All player statistics used for grading are sourced from third-party sports data providers. BattlePicks does not independently compile or verify player statistics. In the rare event of a discrepancy between data providers, BattlePicks will use its reasonable judgment to determine the correct result. BattlePicks' determination of Contest results is final.
